About Me

I am Kenneth Otieno Onditi, a researcher specializing in molecular ecology and evolution, and everything in between. My work focuses on small mammals in vulnerable ecosystems of the Aftropics.

I hold a PhD in Zoology from the Kunming Institute of Zoology, Chinese Academy of Sciences.

Research Interests

  • Ecological and evolutionary patterns and processes
  • Taxonomy and phylogenetics of small mammals, particularly rodents
  • Functional diversity and elevational diversity gradients
  • Human impacts on ecosystems and conservation genetics
  • Eco-evolutionary implications of environmental gradients

Selected Publications

  • Elevational diversity gradients and functional diversity in small mammals - BMC Ecology and Evolution
  • Taxonomy and divergence of Lophuromys aquilus complex - Zoological Journal of the Linnean Society
  • Community ecology and conservation biology - Ecological Indicators
